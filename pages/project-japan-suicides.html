Project Summary

What This Is

  • End-to-end public health data engineering pipeline
  • 46 years of Japan suicide statistics unified and cleaned
  • Economic impact and policy scenario analysis

Key Insights

  • Working-age groups (20–59) dominate economic loss
  • Annual productivity loss exceeds ¥300B
  • Targeted interventions show strong ROI potential
Stack: Python, Pandas, BeautifulSoup, Plotly, NPV modeling
Focus: Data engineering, analytics, public policy insight

Interactive Data Exploration

Interactive charts — hover, zoom, and filter to explore long-term trends and policy scenarios.

Approach

Raw suicide statistics were scraped from Japanese police publications, cleaned, normalized, and merged across age, gender, and cause categories. Analysis focuses on long-term trends, economic impact, and policy-relevant insights.

Modeling Note

Forecasting methods (linear regression, ARIMA, gradient boosting) were tested, but limited data volume and volatility produced unreliable results. The project therefore emphasizes descriptive analytics and economic modeling, where conclusions are statistically sound and interpretable.

Results

Working-age populations account for the majority of economic loss. Scenario analysis shows that even modest reductions in suicide rates yield multi-billion yen savings annually.
