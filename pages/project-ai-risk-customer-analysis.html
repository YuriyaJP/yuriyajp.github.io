Health Claim Perception and Consumer Risk: Meal Replacement Case Study

Work in Progress NLP, Consumer Risk Analysis

Project Overview

This project analyzes health-related narratives expressed in meal replacement product reviews published on the YouTube video platform.

The dataset includes 684 comments across 21 videos from 2020‚Äì2025, with 24% discussing health effects ranging from mild digestive issues to more severe signals.

About 24% of comments mention health-related effects, including mild digestive issues, allergic reactions, and weight/metabolic concerns. This indicates that a non-negligible portion of users experience or perceive adverse effects, which could impact brand reputation and trust if ignored. They should monitor these signals and consider reformulation, clearer labeling, or proactive guidance to reduce potential complaints.

The study highlights that automated sentiment and claim analysis can misclassify or amplify risk perceptions. For the business, this is a signal to integrate human oversight in monitoring systems, ensuring that AI-driven insights are interpreted carefully before making marketing or regulatory decisions.
