Health Claim Perception and Consumer Risk: Meal Replacement Case Study

Work in Progress Machine Learning, NLP, Consumer Risk Analysis

Project Overview

This project analyzes health-related narratives expressed in meal replacement product reviews published on the YouTube video platform.

Using video transcripts and accompanying metadata, I examine how automated analytical systems interpret user-reported experiences such as digestion, taste, satiety, and perceived health effects, and where those systems risk misclassification or overgeneralization.

The emphasis is not on evaluating the truth of individual claims, but on identifying patterns, failure modes, and potential harm amplification when consumer health narratives are processed at scale by automated models.

Problem & Context

  • Analyzes publicly available consumer video reviews only
  • Does not provide medical, nutritional, or diagnostic advice
  • Does not assess the clinical validity of health claims
  • Focuses on risks in automated interpretation and aggregation

Methodology

Data Collection

Analysis Framework

The analysis pipeline includes:

  • Claim Extraction: Identifying health-related statements using regex patterns and NLP
  • Sentiment & Intensity Scoring: Evaluating emotional tone and claim strength
  • Risk Classification: Flagging potential misinterpretations or harmful generalizations
  • Aggregation Effects: Studying how automated summaries could amplify bias

Technical Stack

Python, pandas, scikit-learn, Hugging Face Transformers, YouTube Data API v3

Current Status

This is an ongoing, exploratory project. Current work focuses on transcript collection, claim categorization, and early risk analysis of automated interpretation.

Further modeling and evaluation will be added iteratively.
